Thesis:
TLE/SGP4 short-term forecast errors grow quickly and unevenly by orbit type; bootstrap intervals and nonparametric tests give a more faithful uncertainty picture than Gaussian-only summaries.

Audience:
Numerical statistics course defense, instructors and classmates.

One-line arc:
Start from the practical prediction problem, show how historical TLEs become error samples, then prove error growth, uncertainty, and orbit-type differences with statistical evidence.

Slide list:
1. Title: We quantify how fast public TLE/SGP4 forecasts drift.
   Proof object: data-period and sample metric rail.
2. Research problem: The key statistical object is forecast error over 1/3/7 days.
   Proof object: problem statement and contribution stack.
3. Data: 75 targets provide a balanced LEO/MEO/GEO experiment.
   Proof object: sample construction and counts.
4. Method: Each sample pairs an old TLE forecast with a newer TLE reference.
   Proof object: method pipeline with formula.
5. Results overview: GEO has the largest typical error, while LEO has the longest tail.
   Proof object: summary statistics table.
6. Distribution: Mean and RMSE are pulled upward by extreme forecast failures.
   Proof object: boxplot and log distribution figures.
7. Growth law: Median error grows approximately as a power law.
   Proof object: growth curve and fitted exponent table.
8. Bootstrap: BCa intervals expose asymmetric uncertainty under long-tailed errors.
   Proof object: CI comparison figure and interval table.
9. Group tests: Orbit type differences are statistically significant at every horizon.
   Proof object: Kruskal-Wallis and pairwise comparison matrix.
10. Covariance: Empirical covariance provides the matrix form of uncertainty growth.
    Proof object: covariance propagation equation and output file map.
11. Discussion: The long tail is not a nuisance; it is the operational risk.
    Proof object: cause/limitation matrix.
12. Conclusion: The project delivers an end-to-end statistical pipeline and clear orbit-type findings.
    Proof object: three key takeaways and Q&A.
